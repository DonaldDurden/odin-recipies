Swedish Meatballs

Six Portions

Meatballs

  • 2 tablespoons of butter
  • 1/2 yellow onion finely chopped
  • 1 1/2 teaspoons fine salt
  • 1/4 cup of milk
  • 2 large eggs
  • 1/3 cup plain bread crumbs
  • 3/4 teaspoons freshly ground blackpepper
  • 1/4 teaspoon ground nutmeg
  • 1/4 teaspoon ground allspice
  • 1 pound ground beef chuck
  • 1 pound ground pork

Gravy

  • 2 tablespoons butter
  • 3 tablespoons all-purpose flour
  • 3 1/4 cups beef broth
  • 1/2 cup heavy cream
  • 1/2 teaspoon white sugar
  • 1/4 teaspoon Worcestershire sauce
  • salt and freashly ground black pepper to taste

Directions

Step 1

Melt butter in a large skillet over medium heat. Cook and stir onion and salt in butter until onion begins to turn translucent about 6 minutes.

Step 2

Transfer onion mixture to a large bowl. Stir in milk, eggs , bread crumbs, black pepper, nutmeg and allspice.

Step 3

Mix beef and pork into the brad cumb mixture. Cover with plastic wrap and refrigerate for 1 hour

Step 4

Melt butter for gravy in a large skillet over medium heat; whisk in flour and cook until golden brown, 4-5 minutes. Slowly whis in beef broth

Step 5

Increase heat to medium-high, whisk cream into mixture and bring to a simmer. Stir in sugar and Worcestershire. Simmer until slighly thickened, 6-7 min. Season with salt and black pepper. remove from heat and set aside.

Step 6

Preheat the oven to 425F (220 Celsius). Line a baking sheet with aluminium foil and lighly coat with cooking spray.

Step 7

Roll 2 to 3 tablespoons meatball mixture into a ball using wet hands; repeat with remaining meatball mixture. Place meatballs on the prepared baking sheet.

Step 8

Bake in the preheated oven until browned, about 20 minutes. An instant-read thermometer inserted into the center should read at least 160 degrees F (70 degrees C).

Step 9

Transfer meatballs to the gravy and cook over medium-low heat until warmed through, about 5 minutes.
